    <h1 align="center">E101: Riboflavin </h1>
    <p><strong></strong></p>
    <p><strong>E101 (i): Riboflavin <br>
  E101 (ii): Riboflavin- 5-Sodium Phosphate (sometimes referred to as E106) </strong></p>
    <p><strong>Vitamin B2, lactoflavin </strong></p>
    <p><strong></strong></p>
    <p><strong>Origin: <br>
    </strong>Natural colour present in many foods such as milk, eggs, liver and vegetables. Commercially prepared from yeasts. It is also manufactured synthetically. </p>
    <p><strong>Function &amp; characteristics: <br>
    </strong>Yellow food colour. Not very soluble in water. </p>
    <p><strong>Products: <br>
    </strong>Many different products. </p>
    <p><strong>Daily intake: <br>
    </strong>Up to 0.5 mg/kg body weight. </p>
    <p><strong>Side effects: <br>
    </strong>No side effects known in the concentrations used in foods. </p>
    <p><strong>Dietary restrictions: <br>
    </strong>None; E101 can be consumed by all religious groups, vegans and vegetarians. Although it can be produced from milk, this is not done commercially.</p>
